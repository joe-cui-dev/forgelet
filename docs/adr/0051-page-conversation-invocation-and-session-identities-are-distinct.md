# Page Conversation, Invocation, and Session Identities Are Distinct

Browser Workbench generates a stable `conversationId` when a toolbar gesture creates a capture, a fresh `invocationId` for every root, follow-up, or Retry launch attempt, and receives a `sessionId` only after that attempt starts an auditable Session. Protocol frames and persisted panel state carry the relevant identities explicitly rather than deriving one from another. Every root and child Session also records `conversationId`, `invocationId`, `captureId`, `workspaceProfileId`, attempt kind, root and parent identities, and selected output language in its start Trace metadata; `user_task` and normalized final outcome remain the authoritative turn content. This separation lets a Page Conversation exist before its root succeeds, preserves every failed attempt, and keeps Session lineage inspectable without relying on browser storage or transport inference.

# Failed Resume Attempts Leave the Pause in Force

A resume attempt that crashes — a network fault, a provider abort, a process kill — is attempt evidence, not a terminal outcome of the Session: the pause stays in force, the Pause Snapshot survives for retry, and the Trace records the attempt with its own event kind, `session_resume_failed`, instead of `final_summary` and `session_finished`. This follows ADR 0033's own motivation — unattended work must not evaporate on a crash — and closes the gap it left: writing terminal evidence for a non-terminal failure made the Decision Queue's cross-check read a retryable Session as finished and hide it, and made a later successful retry read as failed forever because lifecycle folds assumed at most one terminal event. Widening `session_finished` to also mean "an attempt failed" would change the meaning of an existing event kind, which ADR 0059 forbids; a new kind is the additive path, and it lets `final_summary` and `session_finished` keep their literal meaning of a Session actually ending. The lifecycle fold now derives state from the last lifecycle event — a `session_resume_failed` re-arms the pause, and terminal status comes from the last `session_finished` — which also gives historical traces their honest reading: old crashed-resume traces that recorded a failure still read as failed, and old retried traces read by their final outcome instead of their first failure. Live presentation mirrors the evidence with a `session_resume_failed` live event that tells the user the Session remains paused and how to retry. Alternatives rejected: amending ADR 0033's wording without changing the evidence (records the tension while leaving the queue hiding retryable Sessions), deleting the snapshot on a failed attempt (turns one transient fault into lost unattended work, contradicting ADR 0033), and keeping `session_finished(failed)` as attempt evidence (the queue and session listings keep lying about state that the surviving snapshot proves otherwise).

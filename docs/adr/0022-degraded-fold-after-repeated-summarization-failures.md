# Degraded Fold After Repeated Summarization Failures

ADR 0019 made folds atomic and rejected deterministic dropping of old turns, leaving one failure path unresolved: when the summarization call fails repeatedly, the Session carries the overage forever and eventually dies at the provider on a context-overflow error — a worse audit outcome than any fold. This amends ADR 0019: after a failed fold, the Session carries the overage and retries next turn exactly once; a second consecutive failure triggers a Degraded Fold instead of another retry.

A Degraded Fold is a normal fold whose narrative is a deterministic placeholder (naming the failure count and pointing at the Trace) while the Fact Ledger updates from the folded Observation Digests as usual. This does not reverse ADR 0019's rejection of deterministic dropping, because that rejection was about *silent* drops conflicting with the audit posture: a Degraded Fold is fully traced (folded turn range, failure reason, attempt count), preserves all deterministic facts in the Ledger, and announces its own degradation to the model. Waiting more than two attempts was rejected because each retry risks the main model call dying first on the oversized conversation.
